What is a square foot

A square foot is a unit of area, found absolutely within nations that receive the imperial system and in numerous development and authentic property emerging situations abroad. One square foot by definition is the area of a square the sides of which are one foot long. The idea is strong though so basic. In a product of length and width to obtain the area you calculate and thus get by the lengths made in feet and the finding will be naturally a square footage. The square foot has historical origins in practical measurements of trade, which has a presence in trade prior to that of the modern metric. It is still the default floor area standard used in residential and commercial real estate listings today as well as in building and remodeling plans where the quantity of materials to be used is specified together with labor estimating and code compliance information in square feet.

Even though the concept of area is absolute the square foot is practical in a hands on work since most measurements of a site include the use of a tape measure that has been calibrated in feet and inches. Carpenters paint crews and tilers commonly perform calculations of numbers in square feet, as the itemials including packages and costs of their products are commonly affordable expressed in square feet. As an illustration a flooring box may occupy 20 square feet and therefore you want to know how many square feet of the room you have so that you know how many boxes to purchase. Just as the rate of paint coverage tends to be given through the supply of square feet per gallon to derive quick estimations of paint supply.

You should know how many square feet correspond to square meter when doing comparisons between units, since metric units are used within most of the supplies and other international standards. A one-foot area equals exactly 0.09290304m 2. On the other hand one square meter is equivalent to about 10.7639104 square feet. You will use this conversion factor when comparing prices measured in terms of square meter and those prices that are given in terms of square feet. A precise conversion does not result in expensive errors during ordering of materials that are shipped in parts of the world that have a different unit system.

Practically use square foot to plan layout to estimate quantities, to compare costs of products. It is simply peacemaking and can be directly mapped to the job tool applied site, and most of the supplier costing standardizations. When exact actual than that of engineering level area is mandatory convert to square meter and maintain records of the decimal accuracy. Most square foot rounded to two decimal places for invoice quotes and purchase orders is adequate and easy to read most remodeling and construction work.

How to measure the square footage of a house or property

Measuring the square footage of a house or property is more than just adding the areas of the rooms to know the square footage of a house or piece of property. Gross living area net living area and total floor area have different measurement conventions so that depending on the intent of the measurement you might do either a listing or remodel or create construction documents. In real estate listing many agents use gross living area which is generally finished heated living space calculated externally and measured between exterior wall to exterior wall on a above grade floor or floor. Depending on local practice basements, attics and detached buildings can be treated individually. When construction contracts are involved you might be required to give a figure of the interior floor area that will be used in materials and the footprint area that will be the space the structure will cover on the lot.

You can begin by making a plan or pacing off the house with a tape measure. Take measurements to establish the room length and width each record the measurements and then find the area with either a rectangle formula or decomposition with irregular rooms. Appendix closets in the hallways and other interior areas, depending on the area you correspondingly agreed to. Where each staircase run and landing area part of the floor area calculation is by convention, measure each tread unless it is adjacent to another tread. For exterior footprint include the wall thickness to building permit definition To obtain outside measurements of full dimension.

Existence of additions and dormers and vaulted spaces to a property elucidate the existence of ceiling volume in determining area reporting. The majority of area standards include horizontal floor as opposed to vertical volume meaning that a cathedral ceiling would increase actual square feet when it actually does not. Only floor areas having permanent heat source and finished surfaces should be counted as such even though you need heated floor area. Finished garages can be considered as living space provided that they otherwise comply with the local code conditions but otherwise they will be listed separately.

In large lots calculate the acreage or square meter of the property based on the survey chips or cadasters or using a computerized online mapping system which has the capability of measuring the acreage/square meters. To have both units of acres and square feet, multiply by 43 560. In outdoor measurements needed in landscaping or paving applications use the same decomposition strategy, except to separate out sidewalks driveways and terraces in order to obtain a distinct cost estimated each.

Last, list details of measurements, sketches photographs and notes and store all calculations. This accurate calculation prevents excess supply of materials and generates transparent forecasts in the face of customers. The licensed surveyor or architect should be consulted when in doubt especially of legal descriptions permits or transactions in which an accurate area must be obtained or required by code or title documents.

FAQs

What is the difference between square foot and floor area

A square foot is a unit of area whereas floor area is measured quantity taking the form of a square foot. When professionals are referring to floor area, they are talking about the total amount of floor surfaces as defined by some convention, e.g. gross living area or net usable area. The unit square foot merely means the what the area is numerically. An example is a room that is 150 square foot in area and consideration of this figure helps in making energy estimates of flooring and paint required.

Do I include walls and closets when I measure square footage for a remodel

It is reliant on the scope of work. The material such as flooring, which you often use would be measured using the actual floor area without the wall thickness. In the case of exterior measurements (wall and thus wall-thickness measured) which architects and builders may make in order to compute permits and structural computations, this gives rise to a bigger number. Remember to always identify which measurement convention you are using and it should be clearly stated whether in your estimate you are using area including the thickness of the wall or not.

How much waste allowance should I add when calculating quantity

Waste allowance depends on the material and complexities of installations. An allowance of five per cent would be sufficient in simple tile jobs and ten to fifteen per cent by diagonal tile or patterned wood. Laminate flooring and engineered flooring tend to require percentages of five to ten. Roofing and siding also may require up to ten percent or more due to cutting as well as overlap. Select a permissible tolerance according to the complexity of the material to be used and the experience of the installer. Put the allowance in writing in such a way that the client will comprehend why extra material is being ordered.

Why do my measured areas differ from the listing or from plans

Differences are as a result of measuring convention rounding and exclusion or inclusion of exterior walls garages basements or attic space. Listings can show gross living area which is according to a local standard whereas on site your people may have measured interior dimensions. Plans can be scaled or can contain that dimensioned to the exterior of wall. Get straight by explaining the grounds and one can make exact measurements or employ a professional survey where advanced accuracy is needed.
